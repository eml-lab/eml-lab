Dr. Yoosoon Chang is a distinguished Professor of Economics at Indiana University, specializing in econometrics and machine learning applications in economics. Her groundbreaking work bridges traditional economic analysis with modern computational methods. She has made significant contributions to time series econometrics and financial economics, particularly in developing novel methodologies for analyzing complex economic systems. Her recent research focuses on applying machine learning techniques to understand macroeconomic dynamics and energy markets, leading to innovative approaches in economic forecasting and policy analysis. Dr. Chang has published extensively in top-tier academic journals and has received numerous awards for her research excellence.

Research Interests

Machine Learning, Time Series and Financial Econometrics, Empirical Macroeconomics, Empirical Finance, Energy Economics

Dr. Joon Y. Park is a renowned econometrician and the Wisnewsky Professor of Human Studies at Indiana University. His research has fundamentally shaped modern econometric theory, particularly in nonstationary time series analysis. He has pioneered innovative approaches in functional data analysis and machine learning applications in economics. His work spans theoretical econometrics and practical applications, contributing significantly to our understanding of economic time series and statistical inference. Dr. Park's research has been instrumental in developing new methodologies for analyzing complex economic data, and he has mentored numerous students who have gone on to distinguished careers in academia and industry.

Research Interests

Econometrics, Machine Learning and Functional Data Analysis
